The Six RS Numbers

what each column actually measures
30M
The last 30-minute bar's move, ranked against the market.
1HR
The last hour bar's move, ranked against the market.
INTR
Today's move since the open, ranked. The daytrade number.
5D
The last 5 sessions, ranked. The swing entry read.
20D
Short-term RS — the base under a swing.
252D
Long-term RS — a full year of leadership.

Read 30M and 1HR carefully. They measure the last bar only — they tell you what just moved, not what has been strong. A 95 on 1HR means it popped in the last hour, and it can be back to 20 an hour later. Always pair them with 5D or 20D to know whether the strength is real.

The Screener  ·  /screen

build your own list — every filter is optional
RS Filters

Any timeframe: 30M · 1HR · INTR · 5D · 10D · 20D · 252D. Set a minimum, a maximum, or both to box in a range. Stack as many as you want — they all have to be true.

Day Move

Filter on how far the stock has moved today. A positive number means up at least that much; a negative means down at least that much. Add an upper bound to box it in.

Sector

Narrow to one sector bucket — or leave it blank to search every rated name. The card always prints the size of the pool it searched.

strong on the week, but only just —
/screen d5_min:90 d5_max:92

strong today AND strong on the week AND a real base —
/screen intr_min:90 d5_min:90 d10_min:80

the strongest semis on a 5-day basis —
/screen sector:Semis d5_min:80

everything down 3% or worse today —
/screen day:-3

It will never lie to you with an empty list. If a number you filtered on isn't available yet — 30M and 1HR don't exist until the market's first scan — the bot says so instead of handing back a blank result that looks like "nothing qualifies."

Reading The EDGE Column

on the daytrade scan card — nothing to type

EDGE answers one question on every row: how often did a setup like this run +2% before the close? It's built from months of the scanner's own history, matched on what time it is, how long the name has been on the card, and how big today's move already is.

It is measured from the current scan forward — never from when the name first showed up. A name that's been on the card since 9:35 has already used up most of its runway by 3 PM, and EDGE knows that. That's the whole point of it.

64% N512Strong — 55% or better, and the capital N means a big sample behind it.
49% N347Middling — 45–54%. Close to a coin flip.
38% n64Weak — under 45%. The lowercase n means a thin sample; trust it less.
NO READAfter 3:30 PM there isn't enough runway left for a +2% number to mean anything, so it says nothing rather than mislead you.

Lookup Commands

pull live data on any ticker
/rs TICKER
The core command. Every RS timeframe on one name — long-term, short-term, weekly, intraday, hourly and half-hourly — plus liquidity grade, bench/heating status, and a trend arrow showing whether it's strengthening.
e.g. /rs NVDA
/rschart TICKER timeframe UPDATED
Fourteen candles with the RS on every one. Defaults to 30-minute; switch to 1-hour or daily. Watch strength build or break bar by bar.
e.g. /rschart NVDA  ·  /rschart NVDA timeframe:1-Hour
/contract TICKER
The highest-volume call and put for the nearest weekly expiry — the contracts actually being traded most right now.
e.g. /contract HIMS
/earnings TICKER
The next earnings date and how many days away it is — so you don't get caught holding a swing into a surprise report.
e.g. /earnings AMD
/bench
The current Active Bench — the strongest long-term and short-term leaders the scanner is tracking right now.
no ticker needed
/heating
The Heating Up list — names whose short-term momentum is accelerating away from their long-term base. The early-ignition radar for swing runners.
no ticker needed

Personal Watchlists

get pinged when your stocks hit the scanner

Two watch types — pick the one that matches how you trade

DAYTRADE WATCH /watchday

Pings you when your stock shows up as a strong intraday call candidate — for same-day and short-hold momentum plays. Checked every few minutes during market hours.

triggers: top-20 calls  ·  intraday RS ≥ 90  ·  weekly RS ≥ 60
SWING WATCH /watchswing

Pings you when your stock hits the bench or heating up — the signal it has become a genuine market leader worth a multi-day swing.

triggers: makes the bench  ·  LT RS ≥ 80  ·  ST RS ≥ 80
/watchday TICKER
Add a stock to your daytrade watch. If it isn't in the scanner yet, the bot validates it and adds it automatically. Up to 15 names.
e.g. /watchday NBIS
/watchswing TICKER
Add a stock to your swing watch. Pings you when it becomes a bench-level leader. Up to 15 names.
e.g. /watchswing PLTR
/mylist
Shows both your daytrade and swing watchlists in one place.
no ticker needed
/unwatch TICKER
Removes a stock from your watchlists. Use the scope option to clear day, swing, or all at once.
e.g. /unwatch NBIS
/mute
Keep your lists but silence all watch pings temporarily.
no ticker needed
/unmute
Turn your watch pings back on.
no ticker needed
