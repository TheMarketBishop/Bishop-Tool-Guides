What's New

newest commands & scan upgrades
/funds
NEW
A whole second scoreboard, just for funds and ETFs — 43 of them, ranked only against each other instead of drowning at the bottom of a stock list. Short-timeframe RS columns
NEW
The scans and list commands now carry 30M · 1HR · INTR · 5D alongside the long-term and short-term numbers. You can finally see whether a name is strong right now or just strong on paper — a stock that's been a leader all month but is bleeding on the hour reads very differently from one that's firing on every clock.
/sectorheatmap  ·  /sectorrank
NEW
Two new ways to read the whole board at a glance. The heatmap paints every sector as a coloured block across six timeframes — bigger and brighter means stronger. The rank card goes one level deeper and ranks names inside their real industry group, so a chip stock is measured against other chip stocks, not against a utility.
STRONG MONTH tag on the call posts
NEW
When a name has had a genuinely strong month behind it, it now gets pulled onto the posted call card and wears a cyan STRONG MONTH tag. Read this correctly: it adds names, it never removes any. Nothing is filtered out because of it. It's a flag that says this one has a strong month underneath the move — historically the best single tell on the call side.
Bench prices are live all day
FIXED
The saved bench list used to hold the morning's price all day, so pulling it up at 3 PM could show a number that was hours old — sometimes 7% away from reality. It now carries the same price as the card that was just posted, and if a fresh price genuinely isn't available it shows a dash instead of quietly showing you a stale one.
Bigger universe, proper sector labels
UPDATED
974 names are now tracked — 33 added this week alone. On top of that, 59 names that had no sector at all now carry a proper sector and industry label, so they finally show up where they belong in the sector tools instead of falling through the cracks.
Weekend mode
UPDATED
Pull any command over the weekend and you now get the last trading day's ratings instead of blanks — so you can do your Sunday homework on the same numbers the market closed on.

Lookup Commands

pull live data on any ticker
/rs TICKER
The core command. A stock's long-term and short-term RS, its short-timeframe readings, liquidity grade and bench/heating status — with a trend arrow showing whether it's strengthening or fading.
e.g. /rs NVDA
/rschart TICKER
A price chart with the RS story on it — see how a name's strength moved alongside the stock itself. Flip between intraday and daily. Untracked names render honestly with dashes rather than made-up numbers.
e.g. /rschart PLTR
/contract TICKER
The highest-volume call and put for the nearest weekly expiry — the contracts actually being traded most right now.
e.g. /contract HIMS
/earnings TICKER
The next earnings date and how many days away it is — so you don't get caught holding into a surprise report.
e.g. /earnings AMD
/bench
The current Active Bench — the strongest long-term and short-term leaders the scanner is tracking right now.
no ticker needed
/heating
The Heating Up list — names whose short-term momentum is accelerating away from their long-term base. The early-ignition radar for potential swing runners.

The Fund Board

brand new — funds ranked against funds

Why funds get their own scoreboard

A FUND IS A BASKET the problem

A fund holds dozens of stocks at once, so it moves less than any single name inside it. Thrown in with 974 stocks it would sit near the bottom forever and you'd never see it — which is exactly what was happening.

SO THEY RACE EACH OTHER the fix

Funds are now scored only against other funds. Same ratings you already read, separate scoreboard — so the strongest corner of the market rises to the top of its own list instead of drowning in the stock rankings.

43 funds  ·  sectors, semis, biotech, metals, energy, bonds, crypto  ·  refreshed every weekday evening
/funds NEW
The full fund board, ranked. Every fund's strength across the short and long timeframes, what it actually holds in plain English, price, day change and liquidity. SPY, QQQ and IWM are in the list carrying a REF badge — they're the yardstick you measure the rest against, so if a sector fund is sitting above them it's genuinely leading the market, not just moving. They are reference lines, not trade ideas.
no ticker needed

Personal Watchlists

get pinged when your stocks hit the scanner

Two watch types — pick the one that matches how you trade

DAYTRADE WATCH /watchday

Pings you when your stock shows up as a strong intraday call candidate — for same-day and short-hold momentum plays. Checked every few minutes during market hours.

fires when: it lands on the live daytrade call board
SWING WATCH /watchswing

Pings you when your stock hits the bench or the heating list — the signal that it has become a genuine market leader worth a multi-day swing.

fires when: it joins the bench or heating up

On The Scans — Nothing To Type

things you'll see on the posted cards

These run automatically on every scan the bot posts. You don't type anything — just know what you're looking at.

STRONG MONTH tag

A name with a strong month behind it gets pulled onto the call card wearing a cyan tag. It adds names, never removes them.

IVR column

Swing scans show an IVR column — green means cheap options, orange means rich premium. A quick read on whether you're overpaying for a breakout.

Earnings badges

Swing scans flag any bench name within about a week of earnings — so a multi-day swing doesn't walk into a surprise report.

An honest dash

If a number genuinely isn't available, the card shows a dash. It will never quietly show you a zero or a stale figure dressed up as live data.

Separate swing channel

Swing scans post in their own channel, apart from the daytrade scans, so each stream stays clean.

Powered by Unusual Whales

Every scan image carries the data-source credit — the live options, RS and earnings data run on the Unusual Whales API.
